# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Visitors who arrive at `www.loamly.net` and need to understand what Loamly is before entering a project. They may be anime fans, collaborators, creators, technical partners, investors, or candidates who want a credible signal that Loamly is more than a single subdomain.

## Product Purpose
Loamly is the official company site and project hub for a network of living internet worlds. The homepage should make Anime immediately reachable, explain the broader company ambition in a polished way, show CEO Kris Kai and CTO Lewis Luo as leadership signals, provide contact paths, and invite like-minded collaborators through a Career section.

## Brand Personality
Controlled, cinematic, precise. The interface should feel like a private future-media company operating from a dark signal room: premium, restrained, slightly dangerous, and not generic SaaS.

## Anti-references
Avoid busy neon overload, generic SaaS cards, fake dashboard chrome, emoji-led project tiles, fake traction claims, placeholder links that appear active before a project exists, and a bland project-directory-only page that undersells the company.

## Design Principles
- Make Loamly feel larger than a project link while keeping Anime immediately reachable.
- Use company-grade narrative: confident, high-context, slightly mythic, but not factually misleading.
- Let the CEO image carry cinematic authority without turning the page into a personal portfolio.
- Support English, Japanese, and Chinese as first-class site languages.
- Keep the page static, fast, and easy to deploy on `www.loamly.net`.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-visible focus states, reduced-motion alternatives for animated visuals, and touch targets of at least 44px. Language switching must use semantic buttons and update the page language for English, Japanese, and Chinese. Color is used for atmosphere, not as the only way to identify active or reserved projects.
